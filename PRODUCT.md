# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Engineering leads and hiring managers evaluating Tulio for full-stack or Apple-platform work. Apple-platform developers, design-engineering peers, and technical readers who follow the blog for native apps, systems thinking, accessibility, and craft. Visitors arrive from GitHub, social profiles, referrals, or search; they are usually scanning for credibility, depth, and a clear path to contact or subscribe.

## Product Purpose

Tulio Cunha's personal site is a portfolio and publishing platform: projects, long-form writing, an about narrative, contact, and newsletter. It exists to establish expert credibility as a full-stack and Apple-platform engineer, surface real work and thinking, and convert interest into conversations, collaborations, or repeat readership. Success looks like a memorable first impression, engaged return readers, and inbound opportunities that match the work shown.

## Brand Personality

Voice is direct, systems-minded, and confident without hype. The interface should feel like Apple Developer documentation crossed with macOS Tahoe: dark, layered, material-aware, precise. Copy favors specifics over slogans; the craft of building is part of the message, not wallpaper behind it.

Three words: **precise**, **native**, **grounded**.

Emotional goals: trust through competence, quiet delight through motion and materials, calm focus for reading.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages: cream or sand body backgrounds, hero-metric blocks, identical icon-card grids, gradient text, uppercase eyebrows on every section.
- Marketing buzzword voice ("streamline", "empower", "seamless", "world-class").
- Playful startup illustration aesthetics or loud gradient hero treatments.
- Decorative glassmorphism and visual noise that competes with content.
- Portfolio templates that look assembled rather than engineered.

Positive anchors already in the codebase: Apple HIG token system, SF Pro typography, liquid-glass navigation, macOS-style elevation, editorial blog surfaces.

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach** — The site must demonstrate the engineering and design standards described in the writing. Performance, accessibility, and structure are part of the portfolio.
2. **Show the system** — Depth, materials, and motion communicate craft before copy does. Surfaces should feel intentional, not templated.
3. **Internals-first clarity** — Information hierarchy and navigation should be as deliberate as backend architecture. Read the system before decorating it.
4. **Accessibility as constraint** — WCAG compliance, reduced motion, and readable contrast are design inputs from the start, not polish passes.
5. **Restraint over decoration** — Every blur, shadow, and animation earns its place. Prefer one strong material language over stacked effects.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA** for text contrast, focus visibility, and interactive targets. The codebase already implements reduced-motion paths and semantic color tokens; preserve and extend those patterns. Avoid color-only state communication. Blog and long-form surfaces need comfortable reading measure, clear heading hierarchy, and link text that stands alone out of context.
